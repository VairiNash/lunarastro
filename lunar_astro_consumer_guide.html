Consumer Protection Guide

How to Protect Yourself from Astrology Scams & File Complaints

The rise of "GuruPreneurs" in the belief-based economy requires consumers to be highly vigilant. If you are seeking astrological or spiritual guidance, follow these steps to protect yourself from predatory patterns, bait-and-switch tactics, and digital fraud.

1 Avoid High-Value Prepayments

Be extremely wary of paying large sums (e.g., ₹10,000 to ₹2 Lakhs) for consultations, "remedies," or courses upfront. Scammers often use "Digital Scarcity" (claiming limited seats or closing doors) to pressure you into immediate payment.

2 Research Beyond Google & YouTube

Look for reviews on independent platforms like MouthShut, Reddit, or independent forums. Scammers often use "domain flooding" and coordinated "troll farms" to manipulate Google Reviews and YouTube comments, burying negative feedback.

3 Demand Transparency (Avoid the Bait-and-Switch)

Ensure you know exactly who will be conducting your consultation. Do not accept tactics where you pay a premium for a master but receive an anonymous student operating under a code (e.g., Astrologer #5007).

4 Initiate Bank Chargebacks

Victims of bait-and-switch tactics or low-quality services report success by initiating chargebacks through their banks or credit card providers. When filing the dispute, cite "service not provided as described."

5 File Official Complaints (India)

If you have been defrauded, use platforms like India’s National Consumer Helpline (INGRAM) to file formal grievances against deceptive marketing and refund evasion. You can also verify the company's legal standing on the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) portal.
