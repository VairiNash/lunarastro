Detailed Forensic Breakdown

1. The Educational Discrepancy: Electrical vs. Robotics

Deepanshu Giri frequently leverages the title of "Robotics Engineer" to project an aura of high intelligence, scientific rigor, and analytical superiority. This is a calculated psychological anchor designed to make his astrological predictions appear mathematically infallible. However, his own professional footprint reveals he holds a degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering. While he operated underwater ROVs (Remotely Operated Vehicles) as a supervisor, conflating an equipment operator/electrical background with a formal degree in Robotics Engineering is a deliberate exaggeration of credentials.

2. The "7 Generations" Myth & The Sunil John Betrayal

To establish traditional authority, Lunar Astro marketing materials claim Giri hails from a 7-generation lineage of astrologers and Ayurvedic practitioners. This directly contradicts his other marketing narrative: the struggling engineer who discovered astrology during a period of severe depression and career failure in 2017. Furthermore, his claim of being "self-taught" or having "decoded" ancient texts is entirely false. Digital archives and consumer testimonies confirm he learned his foundational techniques from Sunil John of Saptarishis Astrology. He appropriated the Bhrigu Chakra Paddhati (BCP) technique, rebranded it as "Paribhraman Paddhati," and subsequently erased all acknowledgments of his teacher from his website.

3. The Consultation Bait-and-Switch (The "Code 5007" Protocol)

Perhaps the most financially damaging claim is the promise of expert, 1-on-1 consultations. Lunar Astro utilizes Deepanshu Giri's YouTube persona as a top-of-funnel marketing tool. Consumers pay exorbitant fees expecting his expertise. Instead, they are subjected to a bait-and-switch tactic where anonymous proxy students—identified only by numbers like "Code 5007"—conduct the readings. Verified reviews on Trustpilot and MouthShut describe these proxy readings as "highly negative," "focused on fear," and delivered by "inexperienced" individuals whose primary goal appears to be upselling further remedies.
